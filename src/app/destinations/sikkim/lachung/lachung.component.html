Welcome to Lachung: The Jewel of North Sikkim

Discover the serene beauty of Lachung, a picturesque mountain village surrounded by snow-capped peaks, rhododendron forests, and cascading waterfalls.

About Lachung

Lachung is a beautiful mountain village in North Sikkim, situated at an altitude of 8,610 feet. Known for its natural beauty and tranquil atmosphere, it is surrounded by snow-covered mountains, flowing waterfalls, and vibrant rhododendron forests. Lachung is also the gateway to the famous Yumthang Valley and is home to the culturally rich Lachungpa tribe.

Why Visit Lachung?

Breathtaking Landscapes

Towering mountains, glacial rivers, and verdant valleys make Lachung a visual treat.

Cultural Richness

Explore the Lachung Monastery and experience the traditions of the Lachungpa people.

Gateway to Yumthang Valley

Known as the "Valley of Flowers," Yumthang is just a short drive from Lachung.

Seasonal Beauty

Enjoy snow-covered vistas in winter, blooming rhododendrons in spring, and lush greenery during the monsoon.

Top Attractions in Lachung

Lachung Monastery

Built in 1880, this spiritual haven is surrounded by picturesque landscapes and hosts vibrant religious festivals.

Yumthang Valley

A paradise for nature lovers, known for its rhododendrons and alpine flowers.

Zero Point (Yumesamdong)

Located at 15,300 feet, this is the last motorable point near the Indo-China border.

Chopta Valley

People know Chopta Valley for its trekking paths, beautiful plants, and clear rivers. It is a hidden gem near Lachung. This place offers both peace and adventure.

Bhima Nala Falls

Also called the Amitabh Bachchan Falls because of its great height, this waterfall is a beautiful natural site. It attracts visitors with its loud, rushing water.

Hot Springs near Yumthang Valley

Relax in these natural hot springs believed to have medicinal properties. A great place to relax in nature exists.

Shingba Rhododendron Sanctuary

Home to over 40 species of rhododendrons, this sanctuary is a delight for photographers and nature enthusiasts.

Things to Do in Lachung

Local Interaction

Explore trails to Tendong Hill and Maenam Hill.

Bird Watching

Enjoy panoramic views of Kanchenjunga.

River Rafting

Enjoy rafting in the icy waters of the Lachung River.

How to Reach Lachung

By Air

The nearest airport is Pakyong Airport (120 km), but Bagdogra Airport (190 km) offers better connectivity.

By Train

The closest railway station is New Jalpaiguri (NJP), approximately 190 km away.

By Road

Lachung is well-connected by road to Gangtok (120 km). Shared or private taxis are the most common means of transport.

Best Time to Visit Lachung

The ideal time to visit Lachung depends on the experience you seek:

  • Spring (March to May): Blooming rhododendrons and pleasant weather.
  • Summer (June to August): Lush greenery and cascading waterfalls.
  • Autumn (September to November): Clear skies and panoramic views.
  • Winter (December to January): Snowfall and snow-covered landscapes.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Is Lachung suitable for a family trip?

Yes, Lachung is a family-friendly destination offering activities and attractions for all age groups.

Do I need to carry cash in Lachung?

It is advisable to carry cash, as ATMs and card payment facilities are limited in this remote area.

Are permits required to visit Lachung?

Yes, permits are mandatory as Lachung lies near the Indo-China border. Your travel agent can help arrange them.

What kind of clothing should I pack for Lachung?

Carry warm clothing, especially during winter, as temperatures can drop significantly. Comfortable trekking shoes are also recommended.

Are there medical facilities in Lachung?

Basic medical facilities are available in Lachung, but it is advisable to carry necessary medications.

Tips for Travelers

  • Always check weather conditions before planning your trip to Lachung.
  • Avoid traveling during heavy monsoons because of the risk of landslides.
  • Carry a reusable water bottle to support eco-friendly practices.
  • Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Hire experienced drivers for a safe journey through mountainous terrain.
