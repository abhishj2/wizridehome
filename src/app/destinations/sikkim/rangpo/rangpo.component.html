About Rangpo

Rangpo, often called the Gateway to Sikkim, is a vibrant border town located on the banks of the Teesta River. It is the first entry point into Sikkim for travelers coming from Siliguri, Bagdogra, or NJP. Famous for its bustling bazaar, river views, and nearby tea estates, Rangpo is both a commercial hub and a stopover before exploring East and North Sikkim.

Places to Visit in and Around Rangpo

Teesta River Viewpoint

Perfect for photography and river rafting activities.

Bhalukhop Viewpoint

Offers panoramic views of Rangpo and the Teesta valley.

Rangpo Bazaar

Local markets for shopping handicrafts, fresh produce, and traditional wear.

Aritar Lake (Lampokhari)

Just an hour away, famous for boating and lush landscapes.

Singtam & Majitar

Nearby towns with monasteries, tea gardens, and eco-parks.

Best Time to Visit Rangpo

  • Summer (March–June): Pleasant weather, ideal for sightseeing.
  • Monsoon (July–September): Lush greenery, though heavy rains may disrupt travel.
  • Winter (October–February): Crisp mountain views and perfect for onward trips to Gangtok.

Where to Stay in Rangpo

Rangpo offers budget hotels, guesthouses, and riverside resorts. Many travelers prefer staying overnight in Rangpo before heading deeper into Sikkim.

How to Reach Rangpo

By Air

Bagdogra Airport (about 85 km). Shared or Reserved cabs available with Wizzride.

By Train

Nearest major station: New Jalpaiguri (NJP), 45 km.

By Road

Well-connected via Siliguri–Gangtok Highway (NH10).

FAQs About Rangpo

How far is Rangpo from Siliguri?

Rangpo is about 70 km from Siliguri and takes around 2.5 hours by cab.

Can I book a Shared Cab to Rangpo with Wizzride?

Yes, Wizzride offers both Shared and Reserved cabs from Siliguri, Bagdogra, and NJP to Rangpo.

Is Rangpo worth staying overnight?

Yes, many travelers stay in Rangpo as a peaceful stopover before exploring Gangtok or East Sikkim.

What is Rangpo famous for?

Rangpo is known as the Gateway to Sikkim, its riverside views, tea gardens, and bustling bazaar.

What documents are required to enter Sikkim via Rangpo?

Indian tourists need valid ID proof, while foreign nationals require a Restricted Area Permit.
