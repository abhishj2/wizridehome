About Ravangla

Ravangla is a picturesque town in South Sikkim, nestled at an altitude of 7,000 ft between Maenam and Tendong Hills. Famous for its breathtaking views of the Himalayas, monasteries, and cultural centers, Ravangla is also home to the National Institute of Technology (NIT), Ravangla, one of India’s premier engineering institutions. This makes Ravangla not just a tourist hub but also an educational landmark in Sikkim.

Places to Visit in and Around Ravangla

Buddha Park (Tathagata Tsal)

A giant 130 ft Buddha statue surrounded by gardens and monasteries.

Ralong Monastery

A vibrant Tibetan Buddhist monastery rich in murals and culture.

Rayong Sunrise Viewpoint

Stunning sunrise views over Mt. Kanchenjunga.

Maenam Hill

Popular trekking destination with panoramic Himalayan views.

NIT Ravangla Campus

A modern institute in the lap of nature, symbolizing education amidst Himalayan serenity.

Borong Hot Springs

Natural hot springs located a short drive away.

Best Time to Visit Ravangla

  • Summer (March–June): Pleasant weather, clear skies, and best for sightseeing.
  • Monsoon (July–September): Lush greenery, but occasional heavy rainfall.
  • Winter (October–February) Crisp weather with snow-capped views of the Himalayas.
