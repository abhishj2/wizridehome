Welcome to Baba Mandir: The Sacred Shrine of East Sikkim

Experience the spiritual aura of Baba Harbhajan Singh Mandir, a revered pilgrimage site set amid snow-covered mountains, glacial lakes, and the high-altitude beauty of East Sikkim.

About Baba Mandir

Baba Mandir, located between Jelep La and Nathula Pass, is a sacred temple dedicated to Baba Harbhajan Singh, an Indian Army soldier believed to protect the region even after his passing in 1968. Situated at an altitude of nearly 13,000 ft, this shrine is surrounded by misty valleys, rugged mountains, and pristine lakes.

There are two versions of this temple:
Old Baba Mandir – the original site near his bunker.
New Baba Mandir – the commonly visited one near Kupup due to easier accessibility.

Both hold deep religious and emotional significance for soldiers and civilians alike.

Top Attractions Near Baba Mandir

 

New Baba Mandir (Kupup)

The more accessible shrine with a museum, bunker replicas, and spiritual atmosphere.

Old Baba Mandir

Located closer to Nathula, this is the original site where Baba's bunker was found.

Tsomgo Lake (Changu Lake)

A glacial lake known for its turquoise waters and snowy surroundings.

Kupup Lake (Elephant Lake)

A sacred high-altitude lake shaped like an elephant from above.

Nathula Pass

One of India's highest motorable passes along the Indo-China border.

Memencho Lake

A hidden gem surrounded by forests and mountain ridges.

Best Time to Visit Baba Mandir

  • April to June: Pleasant weather, clear views.
  • October to December: Crisp skies and snow patches.
  • January to March: Heavy snow; road closures possible.
  • Avoid monsoon due to landslides.

Permits for Baba Mandir

A Protected Area Permit (PAP) is mandatory for:

  • Baba Mandir
  • Tsomgo Lake
  • Nathula Pass

Documents Needed:

  • Passport-sized photographs
  • Photocopy of valid ID (Aadhaar, Passport, Voter ID)

These permits are issued only through authorized travel operators.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Is Baba Mandir open throughout the year?

Depends on weather. Winter snow may block roads temporarily.

Is Baba Mandir good for families?

Yes, it's a peaceful and culturally enriching place.

Do I need a permit?

Yes, a PAP is mandatory to visit Baba Mandir.

What should I wear?

Warm clothes, jackets, gloves—weather is cold year-round.

Can I visit Baba Mandir with Tsomgo Lake in one trip?

Yes, both are usually covered together on a day trip.

Tips for Travelers

 
  • Always carry warm clothing—it's cold even in summer.
  • Roads can be steep; choose experienced drivers (Wizzride recommended).
  • If you have breathing issues, avoid overexertion due to high altitude.
  • Keep permits and IDs handy for security checkpoints.
  • Respect the spiritual value and maintain cleanliness.
