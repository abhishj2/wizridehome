Welcome to Aritar Lake: The Hidden Gem of East Sikkim

Discover the peaceful charm of Aritar (Lampokhari) Lake, a serene emerald lake surrounded by dense forests, misty mountains, monasteries, and the quiet beauty of rural Sikkim. Perfect for nature lovers, photographers, and travelers seeking tranquility.

About Aritar Lake

Aritar Lake, also known as Lampokhari Lake, is one of the oldest natural lakes in Sikkim located in East Sikkim’s Rongli subdivision. Situated at an altitude of 5,000 ft, the lake is famous for its peaceful ambiance, boating experiences, surrounding pine forests, and panoramic views of the Himalayan range. The region also offers monasteries, trekking trails, and rich local culture.

Top Attractions in Aritar

 

Lampokhari (Aritar) Lake

A natural lake offering paddle boating, peaceful walks, and beautiful reflections of surrounding forests.

Aritar Monastery (Aritar Gumpa)

One of Sikkim's oldest monasteries known for ancient murals, artifacts, and spiritual serenity.

Mankhim Viewpoint

A spectacular hilltop viewpoint offering sunrise views over Mt. Kanchenjunga and rolling valleys.

Aritar Dak Bungalow (British Bungalow)

A colonial-era heritage house offering old-world charm and stunning valley views.

Evergreen Nursery & Garden

A quiet garden filled with exotic flowers and orchids, perfect for nature photography.

Phushrey Lake

A peaceful lake hidden in the forest, ideal for a short trek and relaxation.

Lungthung & Thambi Viewpoint (Nearby)

Popular high-altitude viewpoints offering breathtaking views of the Zig-Zag (Zuluk) Road.

Kupup Lake (Elephant Lake) – Nearby

A sacred glacial lake shaped like an elephant, accessible via a scenic drive.

How to Reach Aritar

By Air

Nearest Airport: Pakyong Airport (44 km)
Major Connectivity: Bagdogra Airport (122 km)

By Train

Nearest Railway Station: New Jalpaiguri (NJP) – 122 km

By Road

Aritar is well-connected to Gangtok (70 km), Rongli, and nearby towns through shared jeeps and private taxis.

Wizzride reserved cabs can take you directly from Bagdogra, Siliguri, NJP, or Gangtok to Aritar.

Best Time to Visit Aritar

  • Spring (March–May): Clear skies, blooming flowers, great for sightseeing.
  • Summer (June–August): Green landscapes and peaceful weather.
  • Autumn (September–November): Best time for viewpoints and photography.
  • Winter (December–February): Cold weather with misty mornings and crisp mountain air.

Permits for Aritar

Aritar itself usually does not require permits. However, if you plan to continue towards Zuluk, Kupup, or Nathang Valley, a Protected Area Permit (PAP) is required.

Documents Needed:

  • Passport-sized photographs
  • Photocopy of valid ID (Aadhaar, Passport, Voter ID)
