How to Reach Nathula Pass

 

By Air

The nearest airport is Bagdogra (IXB), approximately 125 km from Gangtok. From there, book a Wizzride cab to reach Nathula Pass via Gangtok.

By Train

New Jalpaiguri (NJP) is the closest railway station, around 120 km from Gangtok. Wizzride provides direct transfers from NJP to Gangtok and onward to Nathula Pass.

By Road

Nathula Pass is around 56 km from Gangtok and can be reached by road via Jawaharlal Nehru Road. Wizzride offers shared and reserved cabs for a scenic and safe journey.

Best Time to Visit Nathula Pass

  • Spring (March to May): Ideal for visiting before the snow melts. Clear skies and chilly breezes offer stunning reflections on the lake.
  • Summer (June - August) – Cool temperatures with misty views; occasional showers bring out vibrant alpine flora.
  • Autumn (September - November) – Crisp air, scenic beauty, and snow-covered peaks make it a perfect time for photography.
  • Winter (December - February) – Heavy snowfall transforms the lake into a magical frozen wonderland. Great for snow play and yak rides.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How far is Nathula Pass from Gangtok?

Nathula Pass is approximately 56 km from Gangtok, taking around 2 to 2.5 hours by car depending on road and weather conditions.

Are Wizzride cabs available to Nathula Pass?

Yes, Wizzride offers reserved and shared cab services from Gangtok to Nathula Pass. Pre-booking is recommended, especially during peak seasons.

Is a permit required to visit Nathula Pass?

Yes, Indian citizens require an Protected Area Permit (PAP) to visit Nathula Pass. Foreign nationals are not permitted to visit Nathula Pass due to its proximity to the China border.

Best time to visit Nathula Pass?

The best time to visit is from April to June for pleasant weather and from January to March for snow. Avoid monsoons due to landslide risks.

Are there accommodations near Nathula Pass?

There are no stays directly at the lake, but Gangtok (56 km away) offers a wide range of accommodations including hotels and homestays.

Travel Tips for Tsomgo Lake

 
  • Carry a valid government-issued ID to obtain permits
  • Dress in warm layers—weather can be extremely cold and windy
  • Check the weather forecast; road access may be restricted during snow or landslides
  • Start your trip early in the morning for better visibility and permit processing
  • Don’t litter; help preserve the natural and spiritual sanctity of the lake
