About Cherrapunji

Tucked away in the mesmerizing hills of Meghalaya, Cherrapunji (also called Sohra) is famous for its lush green landscapes, breathtaking waterfalls, mystical caves, and unique natural wonders. Once known as the wettest place on Earth, this picturesque destination offers visitors a mix of adventure, culture, and natural beauty.

TFrom the famous Living Root Bridges to the thunderous Nohkalikai Falls, every corner of Cherrapunji is a marvel waiting to be explored. Whether you’re a nature lover, an adventure enthusiast, or simply seeking a peaceful getaway, Cherrapunji will leave you spellbound.

Top Attractions in Cherrapunji

 

Nohkalikai Falls

At 1,115 feet, Nohkalikai Falls is India’s tallest plunge waterfall, offering breathtaking views amid dramatic cliffs, especially during the monsoon.

Living Root Bridges

The Living Root Bridges in Cherrapunji are crafted from tree roots by the Khasi tribes, with the Double Decker Root Bridge being a must-visit scenic trek.

Mawsmai Cave

Mawsmai Cave near Cherrapunji features limestone formations, narrow passages, and eerie structures for an adventurous underground experience.

Seven Sisters Falls

Also called Nohsngithiang Falls, the Seven Sisters cascade over cliffs and are best seen during the monsoon when they flow in full splendor.

Dainthlen Falls

Set amid lush meadows, Dainthlen Falls is a hidden gem in Cherrapunji, named after a legendary serpent slain by the Khasi people.

Thangkharang Park

Thangkharang Park offers stunning valley views toward Bangladesh’s plains, making it ideal for nature lovers and photographers.

Wei Sawdong Falls – A Hidden Gem

Wei Sawdong Falls, a hidden gem in Meghalaya, features three turquoise tiers and offers a challenging yet rewarding trek.

Cherrapunji’s Local Cuisine – A Taste of Meghalaya

Cherrapunji offers a variety of Khasi delicacies, best enjoyed at local eateries. Must-try dishes include:

  • Jadoh: A Khasi-style pork and rice dish, rich in flavors.
  • Tungrymbai : A fermented soybean dish, unique to Meghalaya.
  • Dohneiiong : Pork cooked with black sesame seeds.
  • Organic Meghalaya Tea : Perfect for the chilly weather.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is the best time to visit Cherrapunji?

Cherrapunji can be visited year-round, but the best time is October to April, when the weather is cool and pleasant. The monsoon (June–September) is ideal for witnessing the waterfalls in full glory.

How far is Cherrapunji from Shillong?

Cherrapunji is about 55 km from Shillong, and the journey takes around 2 hours by road. Wizzride shared cabs provide a convenient way to travel.

Do I need permits to visit Cherrapunji?

No permits are required for Indian tourists. However, foreign nationals need an Inner Line Permit (ILP), which can be obtained online.

What are the best waterfalls to visit in Cherrapunji?

Some of the most stunning waterfalls include Nohkalikai Falls, Seven Sisters Falls, Dainthlen Falls, and Wei Sawdong Falls.
