About Dawki

Dawki, located near the India-Bangladesh border, is surrounded by verdant hills, lush forests, and enchanting waterfalls. Known for its crystal-clear Umngot River, this scenic town offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, adventure, and cultural exploration.

Apart from its natural beauty, Dawki serves as a vital trade route between India and Bangladesh, giving it a unique blend of cultures. Whether you’re seeking tranquility, adventure, or cultural exploration, Dawki offers an unforgettable experience.

Top Attractions in Dawki

 

Umngot River

The Umngot River in Dawki is famous for its crystal-clear waters, offering unforgettable boat rides with views of the riverbed below.

Dawki Suspension Bridge

Built in 1932 by the British, this hanging bridge offers spectacular views of the Umngot River and surrounding landscapes.

India-Bangladesh Border

Dawki’s Tamabil Checkpost marks the India-Bangladesh border, where visitors can witness trade and friendship between the two nations.

Borhill Falls – A Hidden Gem

Borhill Falls, a lesser-known yet stunning waterfall near Dawki, is an ideal spot for a peaceful retreat amidst Meghalaya’s untouched beauty.

Shnongpdeng

Located just 7 km from Dawki, Shnongpdeng is a paradise for adventure lovers. Enjoy boating, snorkeling, kayaking, and ziplining in this breathtaking location.

Mawlynnong

Just 35 km from Dawki, Mawlynnong—“God’s Own Garden”—is famed for its cleanliness, bamboo homes, and lush surroundings.

Dawki’s Local Cuisine – Must-Try Delicacies

Dawki offers a variety of authentic Khasi delicacies that you must try:

  • Jadoh: A Khasi-style pork and rice dish, rich in flavors.
  • Tungrymbai : A fermented soybean dish, unique to Meghalaya.
  • Dohneiiong : A pork salad with onions and spices.
  • Meghalaya-style Momos : Steamed dumplings served with spicy chutney.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is the best time to visit Dawki?

The best time to visit Dawki is between October and April, when the weather is pleasant, and the Umngot River is at its clearest.

How far is Dawki from Shillong?

Dawki is approximately 82 km from Shillong, and the journey takes about 2.5 to 3 hours by road.

Do I need a permit to visit Dawki?

No permits are required for Indian tourists. However, foreign nationals need an Inner Line Permit (ILP), which can be obtained online.

What adventure activities can I do in Dawki?

Dawki offers a range of adventure activities, including boating, kayaking, snorkeling, scuba diving, zip-lining, and camping by the riverside.
