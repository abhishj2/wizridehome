Best Time to Visit Kohima

  • Winter (November - February) – Cool weather and clear skies, perfect for treks and festivals like Hornbill.
  • Spring (March - April) – Blooming flowers and pleasant temperatures ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities.
  • Monsoon (May - September) – Lush greenery, though some trails may be slippery due to rainfall.
  • Autumn (October) – Crisp air and vibrant landscapes, perfect for cultural exploration and nature walks.

Do I need permits to visit Kohima?

Yes, domestic tourists may require an Inner Line Permit (ILP) to visit Nagaland, including Kohima. Foreign tourists need special permits.

Travel Tips for Kohima

  • Carry a valid Inner Line Permit (ILP) for Nagaland entry; apply online in advance if possible.
  • Pack light winter clothes, especially from November to February.
  • Local markets accept cash widely; ATMs are available but may be limited during festivals.
  • Book Wizzride cabs early during Hornbill Festival or peak tourist season.
  • Respect Naga tribal customs, local traditions, and cultural sites during your stay.
