About Ghoom Monastery

Ghoom Monastery, also known as Yiga Choeling Monastery, is one of the oldest and most significant Tibetan Buddhist monasteries in Darjeeling. Located at an altitude of 7,407 feet, it offers a serene spiritual experience amidst the breathtaking Himalayan landscape. This monastery, built in 1850, is home to a magnificent 15-foot statue of Maitreya Buddha (the future Buddha) and features ancient scriptures, Tibetan manuscripts, and colorful murals that reflect the rich Buddhist heritage.

What Makes Ghoom Monastery Special?

  • One of the Oldest Tibetan Monasteries: Built in 1850, steeped in history and spirituality.
  • Magnificent 15-Foot Maitreya Buddha Statue: A centerpiece of devotion and artistry.
  • Ancient Buddhist Texts: Including the 108-volume Kangyur (teachings of Buddha).
  • Beautiful Murals and Tibetan Artwork: Depicting Buddhist teachings and mythology.
  • Tranquil Ambiance: Perfect for meditation and reflection.

FAQs About Ghoom Monastery

What are the visiting hours of Ghoom Monastery?

The monastery is open from 6:00 AM to 6:00 PM every day.

Is there an entry fee for Ghoom Monastery?

Entry is free, but donations for monastery maintenance are welcomed.

How long does it take to explore the monastery?

A typical visit lasts 30 to 45 minutes, but you can stay longer to meditate or explore.

Can I take photographs inside the monastery?

Photography is allowed outside, but you may need special permission for indoor photos.
