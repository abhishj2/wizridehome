Darjeeling: The Queen of the Hills

Experience the charm of Darjeeling, nestled in the Himalayas. Known for its lush tea gardens, colonial-era charm, and stunning views of Mount Kanchenjunga, Darjeeling is a must-visit destination.

About Darjeeling

Darjeeling, often referred to as the Queen of the Hills, is one of India’s most scenic and popular hill stations. Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas in West Bengal, Darjeeling is known for its stunning landscapes, lush tea gardens, colonial-era charm, and the world-renowned Darjeeling Tea. Whether you’re an adventure enthusiast, a nature lover, or looking for a peaceful getaway, Darjeeling has something for everyone.

Top Attractions in Darjeeling

 

Tiger Hill

Witness the mesmerizing sunrise over Mount Kanchenjunga at Tiger Hill, one of India’s most famous sunrise points.

Darjeeling Himalayan Railway

Ride the iconic UNESCO-listed Toy Train through misty mountains and tea gardens.

Batasia Loop and War Memorial

Enjoy breathtaking views of Darjeeling town and snow-capped peaks at Batasia Loop.

Darjeeling Tea Gardens

Visit Happy Valley Tea Estate to witness tea-making and taste fresh Darjeeling Tea.

Ghum Monastery

One of the oldest monasteries in Darjeeling, housing a 15-foot-high Maitreya Buddha statue.

Peace Pagoda

A Buddhist temple offering serene escape with panoramic mountain views.

Adventure Activities in Darjeeling

Darjeeling Toy Train Ride

How to Reach Darjeeling

By Air

The nearest airport is Bagdogra Airport (IXB), located 70 km from Darjeeling. From there, book a Wizzride shared or reserved cab for a comfortable 3-hour journey.

By Train

The nearest railway station is New Jalpaiguri (NJP), about 72 km from Darjeeling. Wizzride offers shared and private cabs to Darjeeling.

By Road

Darjeeling is well-connected to Siliguri, Gangtok, and Kalimpong via winding mountain roads. Shared cabs and reserved cabs with Wizzride ensure a smooth ride.

Famous Local Cuisine – Must-Try Darjeeling Delicacies

 
  • Thukpa: A Tibetan noodle soup with vegetables or meat
  • Momos: The most popular dumplings in Darjeeling.
  • Churpi Soup: A unique soup made from Himalayan cheese.
  • Alu Dum: A spicy potato dish enjoyed with local bread.
  • Darjeeling Tea: The world-famous tea, best tasted fresh from a local tea estate.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is the best time to visit Darjeeling?

The best time to visit Darjeeling is March to May (Spring) and October to December (Autumn-Winter).

How far is Darjeeling from Gangtok?

Darjeeling is approximately 100 km from Gangtok, and it takes around 4 hours by road.

Can I book a shared cab from Bagdogra or NJP to Darjeeling?

Yes! Wizzride offers shared cab services from Bagdogra Airport, NJP, and Siliguri to Darjeeling.

How many days are enough for Darjeeling?

A 3 to 4-day trip is perfect to explore the main attractions.

Are there ATMs and mobile networks available in Darjeeling?

Yes, Darjeeling has multiple ATMs, and all major mobile networks work well in the town.
