About Himalayan Zoological Park

The Himalayan Zoological Park, also known as the Darjeeling Zoo, is a must-visit destination for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers. Located at an altitude of 7,000 feet, the zoo is nestled amidst the lush Himalayan forests and is home to some of the most unique and rare species found in the region. The park offers visitors a rare opportunity to witness endangered species like the Red Panda, Snow Leopard, and Himalayan Monal in their natural habitat.

  • Best Season: March to May (pleasant weather, blooming flowers) and October to December (clear skies and ideal for wildlife spotting).

FAQs About Himalayan Zoological Park

What are the visiting hours of Himalayan Zoological Park?

The park is open from 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM daily.

Is there an entry fee for the zoo?

Yes, there is a nominal entry fee, and there may be additional charges for photography.

Can I spot rare animals in the zoo?

Yes, the park is home to rare and endangered species, including the Red Panda, Snow Leopard, and Himalayan Monal.

How long should I spend at the zoo?

A visit typically lasts between 1 to 2 hours, depending on your interest in wildlife and photography.

How can I reach the zoo from Darjeeling town?

The zoo is just a 3 km drive from Darjeeling and can be easily accessed by Wizzride Reserved Cab or local taxis.
