Kurseong: The Land of White Orchids

About Kurseong

Perched at an altitude of 4,864 ft in the Darjeeling district of West Bengal, Kurseong is a serene hill station fondly called the “Land of White Orchids.” With its tea gardens, heritage sites, and sweeping Himalayan views, this charming town is perfect for travelers seeking peace away from crowded destinations.

Its name, derived from the Lepcha word “Kharsang” (meaning white orchids), reflects the natural beauty of the region. Kurseong also offers a mix of colonial-era architecture, spiritual sites, and lush tea estates.

Places to Visit in Kurseong

Eagle’s Crag – Sunset Point

A famous viewpoint offering breathtaking sunset views of the plains, Siliguri, and the Teesta River.

Netaji Subhash Chandra Bose Museum

A heritage museum showcasing photographs and memorabilia of Netaji’s life.

Ambotia Shiv Mandir

A Shiva temple set amidst scenic tea gardens, ideal for peace seekers.

Makaibari Tea Estate

World-renowned for organic teas, offering guided tours and tea tasting.

Dow Hill & Victoria Boys’ School

Known for colonial charm, pine forests, and interesting local legends.

Deer Park

A family-friendly spot surrounded by greenery, with opportunities to spot deer.

Best Time to Visit Kurseong

  • Spring (Mar–May): Orchids in bloom, ideal for sightseeing.
  • Monsoon (Jun–Sep): Verdant and misty, but expect rain.
  • Autumn (Oct–Nov): Clear skies, perfect
  • Winter (Dec–Feb): Misty and cold, great for cozy trips.
