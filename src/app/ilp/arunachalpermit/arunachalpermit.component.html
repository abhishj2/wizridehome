Inner Line Permit (ILP) for Arunachal Pradesh

Inner Line Permit Guide

Planning a trip to the breathtaking valleys, mountains, and monasteries of Arunachal Pradesh? An Inner Line Permit (ILP) is mandatory for Indian citizens to visit the state.

Who Needs a Permit?

Indian Tourists

All Indian nationals (except residents of Arunachal Pradesh) require an ILP.

Foreign Tourists

Foreign nationals do not need ILP but must obtain a Protected Area Permit (PAP), usually arranged via registered tour operators.

How to Apply for an ILP?

Online Application: You can apply online via the Arunachal Pradesh ILP portal or offline at designated offices such as: Arunachal Bhavan in New Delhi, Arunachal House in Kolkata, Guwahati, Shillong, and Tezpur, Entry check gates within Arunachal Pradesh.
Documents Required: Valid Government Photo ID (Aadhaar, Passport, Voter ID, Driving License) and Two passport-size photographs.
Validity of ILP: A tourist ILP is generally valid for 15 days, extendable at the Deputy Commissioner’s Office in Arunachal Pradesh.
